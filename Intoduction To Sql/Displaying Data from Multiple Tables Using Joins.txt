                   Displaying Data from Multiple Tables Using Joins

Q1.  Write a query for the HR department to produce the addresses of all the departments. Use the EMP and DEPT tables. Show the EMPNO, ENAME,SAL, DNAME and LOC in the output. Use a NATURAL JOIN to produce the results.

ANS:SELECT EMPNO, ENAME, SAL, DNAME, LOC 
    FROM EMP NATURAL JOIN DEPT;


Q2. The HR department needs a report of all employees. Write a query to display the JOB,MGR,SAL,COMM,DNAME of employees whose JOB is SALESMAN.

ANS: SELECT JOB, MGR, SAL, COMM, DNAME
    FROM EMP 
    NATURAL JOIN DEPT 
    WHERE JOB = 'SALESMAN';


Q3.The HR department needs a report of employees in LOC  DALLAS. Display the ENAME, job, DEPTNO, and DNAME for all employees who work in DALLAS.

ANS:SELECT ENAME, JOB, DEPTNO, DNAME   
    FROM EMP 
    NATURAL JOIN DEPT
    WHERE LOC = 'DALLAS';

Q4.Create a report to display employees’ ename and employee number along with their manager’s name and manager number. Label the columns Employee, Emp#, Manager, and Mgr#, respectively.

ANS: SELECT E.ENAME AS "Employee",
     E.EMPNO AS "Emp#",
     M.ENAME AS "Manager",
     M.EMPNO AS "Mgr#"
     FROM EMP E 
     JOIN EMP M ON E.MGR = M.EMPNO;

Q5.Modify the previous Query to display all employees including King, who has no manager. Order the results by the employee number.

ANS: SELECT E.ENAME AS "Employee",
     E.EMPNO AS "Emp#",
     M.ENAME AS "Manager",
     M.EMPNO AS "Mgr#" 
    FROM EMP E 
     LEFT JOIN EMP M ON E.MGR = M.EMPNO
     ORDER BY E.EMPNO;

Q6.The HR department needs a report on job grades and salaries. To familiarize yourself with the SALGRADE table, first show the structure of the SALGRADE table. Then create a query that displays the name, job, department name, salary, and grade for all employees.

ANS: Display the structure of SALGRADE table:
 DESC SALGRADE; 

 Display employee details with salary grade:

 SELECT ENAME, JOB, DNAME, SAL, GRADE 
 FROM EMP 
 NATURAL JOIN DEPT JOIN SALGRADE ON SAL 
 BETWEEN LOSAL AND HISAL;


Q7.Display the ENAME,DNAME of all the employees. Also display those department name which do not have any employees working.

ANS:SELECT ENAME, DNAME 
   FROM EMP
   RIGHT JOIN DEPT ON EMP.DEPTNO = DEPT.DEPTNO;


Q8.The HR department needs to find the names and hire dates for all employees who were hired before their managers, along with their managers’ names and hire dates.

ANS:SELECT E.ENAME, E.HIREDATE,
    M.ENAME, M.HIREDATE  
    FROM EMP E  
    JOIN EMP M ON E.MGR = M.EMPNO 
    WHERE E.HIREDATE < M.HIREDATE;


Q9.Display the EMPNO,ENAME,DNAME,LOC  of those employees who are working as CLERK. Use the USING clause.

ANS:SELECT EMPNO, ENAME,
    DNAME, LOC 
    FROM EMP 
    JOIN DEPT USING (DEPTNO)
    WHERE JOB = 'CLERK';


Q10.Display the ENAME,SAL,MGR,DNAME of employees whose salary is more than 2000. Use the ON clause.

ANS: SELECT ENAME, SAL, MGR, DNAME
     FROM EMP 
     JOIN DEPT ON EMP.DEPTNO = DEPT.DEPTNO 
     WHERE SAL > 2000;


Q11.Display the EMPNO,ENAME,JOB,DEPTNO,DNAME,LOC of employees. Use LEFT OUTER JOIN.

ANS: SELECT EMPNO, ENAME, JOB,
     EMP.DEPTNO, DNAME, LOC 
     FROM EMP 
     LEFT OUTER JOIN DEPT ON EMP.DEPTNO = DEPT.DEPTNO;


Q12.Display the ENAME,DNAME of employees. Use RIGHT OUTER JOIN.

ANS:SELECT ENAME, DNAME 
    FROM EMP  
    RIGHT OUTER JOIN DEPT ON EMP.DEPTNO = DEPT.DEPTNO;


Q13.Display the EMPNO,DNAME,LOC of employees. Use FULL OUTER JOIN.

ANS:SELECT EMPNO, DNAME, LOC
    FROM EMP
    FULL OUTER JOIN DEPT
    ON EMP.DEPTNO = DEPT.DEPTNO;
